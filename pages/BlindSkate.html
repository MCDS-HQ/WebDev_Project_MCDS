About Us!

SNOWTRAIL TRAVELS founded by Lucas Snow has a place for blind skateboarding, due to the fact the owner is blind himself. Blind individuals can excel at skateboarding by relying on their heightened senses and adaptive strategies. Many visually impaired skateboarders develop strong spatial awareness through auditory cues and tactile feedback. They might use sounds, such as the noise of the skateboard wheels on different surfaces or the echoes in their environment, to gauge their speed and proximity to obstacles.

Skateboarding also involves a lot of practice and muscle memory, which can be cultivated through repetition regardless of visual ability. Some blind skateboarders collaborate with sighted partners or coaches who verbally guide them, helping them learn and master different tricks and movements.

Adaptive technologies and supportive communities also play a significant role in this process. Organizations and programs that encourage inclusivity in extreme sports allow blind skateboarders to connect and share experiences, fostering skills and confidence.

As a result, many visually impaired individuals have successfully participated in skateboarding competitions and demonstrations, inspiring others with their skills and determination. Their achievements highlight that passion, perseverance, and adaptability can lead to success in any field, including extreme sports like skateboarding. The company originally operated through a physical headquarters, but with the ever-growing nature of travel, the company eventually expanded to operating digitally in 2024, through the very website you are using now.
